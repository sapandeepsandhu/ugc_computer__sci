Feature Frame Buffer Frame Rate
Definition A storage space in memory for holding pixel data for one frame. The frequency at which consecutive frames are displayed on screen.
Purpose To store the color values for each pixel of an image or scene. To determine how smooth motion appears in video playback or games.
Measurement Measured in terms of resolution (pixels) and color depth (bits). Measured in frames per second (fps).
Dependency Depends on display resolution and color depth. Depends on the graphics system's processing power and complexity of the content.
Role in Video Acts as a container for the current frame to be displayed. Indicates how many frames are displayed per second.
Impact Affects image quality and memory usage. Affects the smoothness and responsiveness of video and animations.
Change Effect Changing size affects memory. A larger buffer allows higher resolution or color depth. Changing rate affects visual fluidity. Higher rates yield smoother motion.
Typical Values E.g., 1920x1080 resolution with 24-bit color depth. E.g., 24, 30, 60, 120, 144 fps, etc.
